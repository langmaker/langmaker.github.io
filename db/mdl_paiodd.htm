Paiodd - Conlang Profile   Advanced
Language NamePaiodd
Language AuthorJeremy Graves
Year Began2000
SitePaiodd
Broken LinkNo
Site LanguageEnglish
Language Typefictional language
UniquenessPaiodd features a unique inflectional system for nouns based on their relationship to verbs or other nouns as opposed to based on their role in the sentence or such things as gender or number. Additionally, these inflectional relationships are demonstrated by different phonetic values of the same phonetic class.
Language SourcesEnglish inflection such as run-ran/strong-strength inspired the stem-changing method of inflection for both verbs and nouns. Also, the language was originally inspired by Latin and Greek, though it has changed dramatically since then. I'm also a fan of the Romance languages, and there are some influences from them.
Design PrinciplesAgain, the nouns feature two inflections: verbal and nominal to indicate which other part of speech they are related too. This eliminates the need for adjectives and adverbs, which are expressed by the respective inflections. Verbs are divided into tense systems and specific tenses.
Interest Of OthersActually, I used Paiodd as a basis for an undergraduate research project in which I described how various types of conlangs can have application to linguistics as a whole. I hope to post that paper as well. I'm fairly certain it was this research that got me into graduate school!
