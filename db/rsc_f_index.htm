Fordsmender's Linguistics Links - I created this list to help others in their conlanging endeavors. These websites are those which have helped me over the years.

Foreign Accent Archive - Recordings of foreign accents. According to the site, "The speech accent archive is established to uniformly exhibit a large set of speech accents from a variety of language backgrounds. Native and non-native speakers of English all read the same English paragraph and are carefully recorded. The archive is constructed as a teaching tool and as a research tool. It is meant to be used by linguists as well as other people who simply wish to listen to and compare the accents of different English speakers."

Form and Function of Rotokas Words - Description of Rotokas, a language spoken in Papua New Guinea by about 3,000 people, famous for its minimal phonology.

Forthright's Phrontistery: Obscure Words and Vocabulary Resources - Strange English words and their definitions.

FREELANG - FREELANG is a useful dictionary program. In it, you may create your own word lists in whatever language you choose or create. This program is very useful for creating a vocabulary quickly and efficiently. It also has practice/test features to help one learn the words. Please note that this is not necessarily its intended function, which is that of an English-to-Whatever dictionary (and vice versa).

Free Language Textbooks from WikiMedia - This site has free textbooks for learning languages that you can study, and also add to, write, and edit. An excellent resource for finding ideas for conlangs for free.
