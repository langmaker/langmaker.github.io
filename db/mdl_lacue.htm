UniquenessLaquish is to a certain degree very compact, and many have also said it sounds very nice (every conlanger's dream). It has few or no irregularities and is mostly agglutinative. Its syntax and morphology is very systematic and once you've understood the basic principles of the language there aren't any real difficulties making new sentences.
Language SourcesI am inspired by a lot of languages but I can't say that Laquish is based mainly on one foreign language. I am myself a native speaker of Norwegian, and I have tried making it sound and feel as foreign as possible from this language - belonging to the Germanic language family. So similarities to German, English and so forth in morphology and syntax are not common! When it comes to the script, I have personalized and modified the Korean alphabet, so it isn't unique in that sense, but still not like the way Koreans use it.
