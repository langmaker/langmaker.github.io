e-waste (n.) - Neologism Profile   Advanced
Worde-waste
Part of Speechn.
Other Formse-wasting (n.)
Etymology[ < electronic & waste ]. Note: coined by California's Electronic Waste Recycling Act of 2003 (SB 20/SB 50).
Coined ByOther (specified in Etymology)
Definition1. Electronic appliances, such as old TV tubes or monitors, containing lead and other metals; e-waste cannot be left in the open without risk of poisoning. 2. The 32 toxic chemicals contained in such items.
Example CitationThe E-waste Act is better known at the cash register, where consumers learn they have to pay a surcharge of up to $10 when they buy a new computer monitor or television. The special tax is meant to fund an ambitious plan to safely recycle old and discarded cathode ray tubes, classified as hazardous waste (Silicon valley.com, May 5, 2005).
Earliest Citation"Since certain components of electronic devices may be considered hazardous due to heavy metal or other constituents, the end-of-life handling of some electronic discards is regulated by either federal (Resource Conservation and Recovery Act--RCRA) or state (Health and Safety Code) hazardous waste laws, or both. New regulations regarding the proper management of Cathode Ray Tubes (CRT) found in computer monitors and televisions were recently approved." - CIWMB
