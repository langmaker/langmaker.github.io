English-Tepa Lexicon

This small lexical index, like all of the materials on Tepa was taken from the journals of Alma Walker (see Sources), and is incomplete in several ways. Because Walker used the Deseret Alphabet in his transcriptions of Tepa, there is still a great deal of decipherment which needs to be done; I will add new lexical items to this index as they are discovered.

Second, Walker was working with a non-native speaker of Tepa. For this reason alone, much of the Tepa language is irretrievably lost. Additionally, it was Walker's practice to gloss Tepa words with only a single English or Southern Paiute word. I have attempted to extrapolate a possible range of meaning for words which occur in narrative contexts as well as in word lists; this has been the exception, however, and not the rule, and I have mostly confined myself to giving Walker's original gloss.

The transcription conventions are the same as in the rest of the grammar; see the Introduction for more details.
